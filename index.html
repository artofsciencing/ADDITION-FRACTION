🧮 Adding & Subtracting Fractions

Learn how to work with fractions step by step!

Section 1 of 5

📖 What You'll Learn

In this lesson, you'll discover how to add and subtract fractions. We'll start with fractions that have the same denominator, then move on to fractions with different denominators.

💡 Remember

A fraction has two parts:

  • Numerator (top number) - tells us how many parts we have
  • Denominator (bottom number) - tells us the total number of equal parts
3 4
← This means 3 out of 4 equal parts

✨ Adding Fractions with Same Denominators

When fractions have the same denominator, adding them is easy! You simply add the numerators and keep the denominator the same.

📝 Example: Adding 2/6 + 3/6

2 6
+
3 6
=
5 6

2 + 3 = 5, so the answer is 5/6

🔑 Key Rule

When denominators are the same: Add the numerators, keep the denominator!

🔄 Different Denominators

When fractions have different denominators, you need to find equivalent fractions with a common denominator first.

📝 Example: Adding 1/2 + 1/3

1

Find a Common Denominator

Find a number that both 2 and 3 divide into evenly. The smallest is 6.

2

Convert 1/2 to sixths

Multiply both numerator and denominator by 3:

1 × 3 2 × 3
=
3 6
3

Convert 1/3 to sixths

Multiply both numerator and denominator by 2:

1 × 2 3 × 2
=
2 6
4

Add the fractions

Now both fractions have the same denominator!

3 6
+
2 6
=
5 6

🔑 Key Steps

  1. Find a common denominator (a number both denominators divide into)
  2. Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with that denominator
  3. Add the numerators, keep the common denominator

➖ Subtracting Fractions

Subtracting fractions works the same way as adding! If denominators are different, find a common denominator first. Then subtract the numerators.

📝 Example: 5/8 - 2/8

5 8
−
2 8
=
3 8

5 - 2 = 3, denominator stays 8
